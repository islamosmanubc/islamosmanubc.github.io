## Problem

Quality control for medical scanners matters clinically, but labeled data can be limited and domain shift is persistent across equipment and acquisition conditions.

## Role

I helped shape the data curation and model-development workflow for automated CT scanner calibration assessment.

## Approach

- Curated relevant medical-imaging data from public and project-specific sources.
- Applied self-supervised pretraining with a vision-transformer backbone.
- Focused on improving domain generalization so the QC model remained useful across varying scanner conditions.
- Evaluated the resulting model against standard baselines to measure practical gain.

## Tools

Vision transformers, self-supervised representation learning, and medical-imaging quality-control modeling.

## Outcome

The model delivered a 12% improvement over standard baselines and supported abstracts, a paper, and patent-oriented work around scanner QC automation.
