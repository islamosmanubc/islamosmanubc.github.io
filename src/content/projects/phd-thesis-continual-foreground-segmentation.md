## Problem

Foreground-segmentation systems often fail when labeled data is scarce or deployment conditions change. Strong performance on a fixed benchmark is not enough if the system cannot adapt while retaining useful knowledge.

## Role

My PhD focused on designing and evaluating methods for robust foreground segmentation under limited-data and continually changing conditions.

## Approach

- Investigated continual and lifelong learning methods for segmentation.
- Explored few-shot learning, self-supervised learning, and transformer-based modeling.
- Focused on preserving prior knowledge while adapting to new scenes and domains.
- Framed the work around practical robustness, not only benchmark performance.

## Tools

Computer vision, continual learning, few-shot learning, transformers, and domain-generalization research workflows.

## Outcome

The thesis produced peer-reviewed papers, conference presentations, and a durable research identity centered on dependable adaptation in real-world vision systems.
